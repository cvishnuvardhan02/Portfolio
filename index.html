Cafe Bliss

"Cafe Bliss" is a website I developed to provide food enthusiasts with an online platform to explore and order a variety of dishes. It features an easy-to-navigate menu with detailed descriptions and prices, a user-friendly ordering system, secure payments, customer reviews, a rating system, and a blog with recipes and culinary tips. The design aims to create a welcoming, cozy café atmosphere.

Climate Change

For the NASA Space Apps Challenge 2024, I worked on a project simulating 50 years of CO2 emissions and global temperature rise to study climate change impacts. Using historical data and climate models, the simulation predicted future temperature trends under different emission scenarios, highlighting the urgency of climate change mitigation. It visualized how varying emission levels could affect global temperatures, contributing to extreme weather, rising sea levels, and biodiversity loss.

Banking Chatbot

The Banking Chatbot project, developed for my AI subject, provides an interactive interface for banking services. It helps customers with tasks like checking balances, transferring funds, making bill payments, and answering service-related queries. Using Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning, the chatbot understands user queries and improves over time, offering a personalized experience. This project enhanced my understanding of AI applications in the banking sector.

Web app

I developed a web application for employee attendance tracking that uses geolocation to ensure attendance is recorded only within 500 meters of the company’s location, preventing fraudulent check-ins. The app features a user-friendly interface for logging in, viewing attendance records, and checking in/out. Built with Node.js, Express, and MongoDB, it securely stores data and improves attendance management efficiency while maintaining system integrity.

OTN

The Organ Transplantation Network project is a DBMS designed to streamline organ donation and transplantation. It connects donors, recipients, and hospitals by storing detailed information on donors, recipients, transplant history, and hospital availability. The system matches compatible donors with recipients based on factors like blood type, medical condition, and location, while managing waiting lists, tracking organ status, and ensuring data privacy.

ORC

The organized robotic car project involved designing a robot that autonomously navigates using sensors and controllers. It featured motors, sensors (e.g., ultrasonic for obstacle detection), and a microcontroller (like Arduino or Raspberry Pi) to process data and control movement. The goal was to enable the car to follow a path, avoid obstacles, and communicate with external systems for tasks like remote control or automated navigation, showcasing robotics and automation in real-world applications.
